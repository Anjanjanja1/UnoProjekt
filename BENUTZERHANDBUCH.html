1. Introduction

Welcome to our digital version of the classic UNO game! This user manual will guide you through the setup, rules, and gameplay mechanics to ensure you have a seamless and enjoyable experience.

2. Game Setup

Players:

  • Supports 2-4 players
  • Choose between human players or bots

Initialization:

  • Enter player names
  • The deck is shuffled automatically
  • Each player receives 7 random cards
  • The remaining cards form the draw pile
  • The top card of the draw pile is flipped over to start the discard pile
  • The first player is randomly selected to start the round

3. User Interface

Main Menu:

  • Displays the current player’s name
  • Shows the top card on the table
  • Lists the player’s cards
  • Highlights possible playable cards
  • Provides options to:
    • Draw a card
    • Play a card
    • Say UNO
    • Pass the turn to the next player
Example:
Player: Anna-Lisa
Top Card: [R3]
Your Cards: [B3 G5 Y2 R6]
Playable Cards: [R6 B3]
MENU:
1. Draw Card
2. Play Card
3. Say UNO
4. Next Player

4. Gameplay

The game checks the top card to determine the next action.

Players take turns in the following sequence:

  • Check if the top card is a Skip, Reverse, +2, or +4 and apply the respective rules.
  • The current player can choose to:
    • Draw a card
    • Play a card
    • Say UNO (when they have only one card left)
    • Pass the turn to the next player
  • The drawn card can be played immediately if it matches the top card.
  • Players must follow the rules for playing cards and handling special action cards.

5. Action Cards

Plus Two Card (+2):

  • Next player draws 2 cards
  • Can be placed on a card of the same color or another +2 card

Reverse Card:

  • Reverses the direction of play
  • Can be placed on a card of the same color or another Reverse card

Skip Card:

  • Skips the next player’s turn
  • Can be placed on a card of the same color or another Skip card

Wild Card:

  • Changes the color to be played next
  • Can be played on any card

Wild +4 Card:

  • Changes the color to be played next and the next player draws 4 cards
  • Can be played on any card

6. Penalties

  • Forgetting to say "UNO!" results in a 2-card penalty
  • Incorrectly played cards must be taken back and a penalty card drawn
  • Attempting to play twice in one turn results in a penalty card

7. Scoring

Each card has a point value based on UNO rules:

  • Number cards (0-9): Face value
  • +2, Reverse, Skip: 20 points
  • Wild, Wild +4: 50 points

The winner of the round scores points based on the remaining cards in other players’ hands

8. Game End

  • The game continues until a player reaches 500 points
  • The player who reaches 500 points first wins the game
  • The winner is announced at the end of the game
